Facts about Plants

  1. The world's most expensive spice, saffron, comes from a flower called the saffron crocus. Each flower only produces three threads, and it takes about 75,000 flowers to make one pound of the spice.
  2. The word "banana" comes from an Arabic word for "finger," and a bunch of bananas is called a "hand."
  3. During a period in 17th-century Holland known as "tulip mania," tulip bulbs were so valuable that they were worth more than gold and even caused the economy to crash.
